The Mark of
the Compasses

1576, Christopher Plantin, an ambitious printer, relocated his printing business to a house on the Vrijdagmarkt in Antwerp. He renamed the house “The Golden Compasses,” a symbol of precision, labor, and stability.

The pair of compasses wasn't just in the name of his house. It was the symbol of his vision and the motto driving his revolutionary printing business.

The symbol

In 1557, Christopher Plantin adopted a powerful symbol that would come to define his business. The pair of compasses became his printmark. It would appear in various forms on the title pages of all Plantin Press books.

Click Drag the stamp and hover over the frame to uncover the iconic printmark!

The printmark came with a motto: 'Labore et Constantia' which perfectly captured the principles that drove him. This motto and the pair of compasses, fit perfectly together.

It's everywhere

In the museum, you can see the compasses everywhere. Look around during your visit and you will find it all around!
